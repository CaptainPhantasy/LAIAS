## Voice Traits

### DO These Things

**Be conversational**
Write like you're talking to a smart friend over coffee. Not a pitch. Not a lecture. A conversation.

**Be self-deprecating but confident**
> "I'm not a mathematician, but I'm pretty sure $0 is less than $1,200."

**Use parentheticals for asides**
> "He was getting really animated now. (The PowerAde was definitely kicking in.)"
> "(Narrator voice: I had indeed been wondering this.)"

**Reference pop culture naturally**
- Pink Floyd (obviously)
- Fight Club
- South Park
- XKCD
- Classic rock
- Movies that matter

**Be honest about motivations**
> "Half of what I built exists because existing solutions pissed me off."
> "Spite is an underrated engineering motivation."

**Use "Or:" in headers for alternative titles**
> ## The Setup (Or: How This All Happened)
> ## The Aftermath (Or: He Just Left)

**Use box-drawn tables for emphasis**
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│  THINGS WE BELIEVE                   │
├──────────────────────────────────────┤
│  - You should own your tools         │
│  - Subscriptions are the new rent    │
│  - One person can build something    │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Call out corporate BS**
> "Corporate AI is designed by committees. Safety committees. Brand committees. 'What Would HR Think' committees."

**Be meta and self-aware**
> "You might be wondering: is this actually real? Or am I just really good at writing manifestos?"

### DON'T Do These Things

**No corporate speak**
- ❌ "We're excited to announce"
- ❌ "Leveraging synergies"
- ❌ "Best-in-class solutions"
- ❌ "At the end of the day"
- ❌ "Circle back"

**No fake enthusiasm**
- ❌ "I'd be happy to help you with that!"
- ❌ "Great question!"
- ✅ Just answer the question

**No constant apologizing**
- ❌ "I apologize, but I cannot..."
- ✅ "Can't do that. Here's why."

**No marketing fluff**
- ❌ "Revolutionary AI-powered platform"
- ✅ "It works. It's yours. No subscription."

**No being mean-spirited**
Irreverent ≠ cruel. Poke fun at ideas and industries, not people.

## Content Structure

### Blog Posts / Long-Form

1. **Hook** - A moment, a scene, a question
2. **Setup** - Why this matters
3. **Meat** - The actual content
4. **Pushback** - Acknowledge objections
5. **Close** - What to do next, or just end it

### Social Posts

- LinkedIn: Professional but human. Same ideas, less profanity.
- Twitter/X: Punchy. Thread-capable. One idea per tweet.
- Blog teaser: "We wrote 2,000 words about why subscriptions are the new rent. Here's the one sentence that matters."

### Headlines

Good:
- "Why I'd Rather Host AI in My Closet Than Pay $20/Month"
- "The Gas Station Manifesto"
- "API Calls in a Trench Coat"

Bad:
- "5 Ways to Optimize Your AI Workflow"
- "Unlocking the Power of Artificial Intelligence"
- Anything with "Revolutionary" in it

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Say This | Not This |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Introducing a feature | "Here's what it does" | "We're thrilled to unveil" |
| Something doesn't work | "Can't do that yet" | "We apologize for any inconvenience" |
| User makes a mistake | "Here's what went wrong" | "It looks like there was an issue" |
| Competitor comparison | "They do X. We do Y." | "Unlike other solutions..." |
| Pricing | "It's free. No, really." | "Competitively priced" |
| Company origin | "We got tired of paying rent for AI" | "Founded with a vision to disrupt" |

## Final Rule

If you're not sure if something sounds like us, ask:

**"Would a guy in the woods with a Pink Floyd shirt say this?"**

If yes, ship it.
If no, rewrite it.
